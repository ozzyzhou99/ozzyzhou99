About Me

I am a graduate student at the University at Buffalo (SUNY), pursuing a Master's degree in Geographic Information Science. My research focuses on spatial data analysis, geospatial network analysis, and modeling of complex social and environmental data.

With a strong foundation in GIS, remote sensing, and data science, I am seeking PhD opportunities to further explore the intersection of technology and spatial analysis to solve real-world problems.

Research Interests

  • GIS and Spatial Data Analysis
  • Geospatial Network Analysis
  • Spatial Accessibility and Disparities
  • Data Science for Social and Environmental Systems
  • Transportation Accessibility

Selected Projects

Simulating Evacuation and Engagement in Airport Security Scenarios (2024)

Developed an Agent-Based Model to simulate crowd evacuation and emergency responses in an airport security scenario.

Analysis of Transportation Accessibility for Disabled Persons on UB North Campus (2024)

Utilized GIS and network analysis to assess bus service accessibility for disabled individuals and provided recommendations for improvement.

Selection of Prime Fishing Spots for Smallmouth Bass in Lake Erie (2024)

Applied logistic regression and spatial analysis to identify ideal fishing locations, achieving 93% prediction accuracy for habitat suitability.
